Nikola Tesla

The man who revolutionized electricity

Here's a time line of Nikola Tesla's life:

  • 1856 - Born as a subject of the Austro-Hungarian Empire in the Balkan Peninsula. To a world that just recently started to use electricty
  • 1866 Has a childhood engrossed in his scholary father's library and immersed in his mother's inventive creations. At the age of 10, Tesla sees an image of Niagara Falls and vows to harness that force of nature.
  • 1873 - Persuades his father, when near death from cholrea, to let him attend the renowned Joanneum polytechnical Schoool in Graz instead of entering the priesthood if he survives.
  • 1876 - Finishes at the top of his class and passes 9 exams in a year where most would take two. Argues with his physics teacher on a different design for electrical transmission that is said to be impossible. Professors write to parents about him overworking and his father in turn criticized him heavily when he returned home.
  • 1878 - Gambles compulsivly and loses his scholarship.
  • 1879 - Tesla's father visits him and tries to persuade him to go back to school but fails.
  • 1880 - Tesla gets arrested, and is deported back to his hometown. His father is heartbroken from seeing this and passes away soon after. Tesla continues gambling and losing money until his mother hands him a roll of cash and tells Telsa to “Go and enjoy yourself. The sooner you lose all we possess the better it will be. I know that you will get over it.”
  • 1881 - Starts to study in University of Prague with funding from uncles. Tesla has to quit later when funding stops and he never receives his diploma.
  • 1882 - Moves to Paris, to work as an engineer for the Continental Edison Company, designing improvements to electric equipment brought overseas from Edison's ideas.
  • 1884 - Moves to Manhattan, United States to manage the Edison Machine Works.
  • 1885 - Nikola Tesla quits working for Edison.
  • 1886 - Nikola Tesla forms Tesla Electric Light and Manufacturing Company.
  • 1887 - War of Currents era begins.
  • 1888 - Tesla Delivers Lecture: A New System of Alternating Current Motors and Transformers.
  • 1891 - Nikola Tesla becomes a citizen of the United States.
  • 1893 - Nikola Tesla and George Westinghouse power the World's Columbian Exposition.
  • 1896 - Nikola Tesla was able to harness the power of Niagara falls to power New York City.
  • 1898 - Nikola Tesla was able to harness the power of Niagara falls to power New York City.
  • 1901 - Construction begins on Tesla's Wardenclyffe Tower to transmit electricity without powerlines.
  • 1905 - Runs out of money from patents expiring and the Wordenclyffe Tower is never finished.
  • 1943 - dies at age 86.
